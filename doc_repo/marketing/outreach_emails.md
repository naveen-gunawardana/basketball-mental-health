**Subject:** Peer Mentorship for Student-Athletes — Mental Health Service Hours

Hi Michael,

I'm a high school student in San Francisco building a platform called Mentality Sports. It's a peer mentorship program that connects college and former athletes with high school athletes for weekly one-on-one sessions focused on the mental side of sport, things like confidence, anxiety, handling a bad stretch, identity after injury.

The idea came from my own experience as an athlete and honestly from not having anyone to talk to about that stuff growing up. Mentality Sports isn't a therapy app or a clinical service. It's just athletes helping athletes through things they've already been through.

I reached out to USF because I saw that your SAAC partnered with UnlimitMe last fall. That kind of openness to mental health programs is exactly what I was hoping to find. Mentality Sports is different from something like UnlimitMe though. There's no therapist involved. It's athlete-to-athlete, informal, and built around the idea that the best person to talk to about being a college athlete is someone who just was one.

For your student-athletes, mentoring through Mentality Sports counts as documented community service hours. Every session is logged and exportable. And since Division I schools are now required to attest they provide peer-to-peer mental health support, it's also a way to check that box without adding anything to your staff's plate.

There's no fee for the athletic department. This is a mission-driven project, not a paid service.
