# College Athletic Partnership Strategy

## The Opportunity

NCAA Division I schools are now required to attest they provide mental health support including peer-to-peer mentorship (NCAA Mental Health Best Practices, August 2024). Mentality Sports is a turnkey solution for that requirement. Frame every outreach around this.

The Princeton "Student-Athlete Wellness Leaders" (SAWLs) model is the closest existing precedent — varsity athletes trained to support peers on mental health, formally recognized by both Athletics and University Health Services. That's what Mentality Sports does, at scale, across schools.

## The Pitch Framework

**For Athletic Directors / Student Development staff:**
> "Mentality Sports is a Peer Wellness Leader program that satisfies your NCAA Division I mental health attestation requirement. Your athletes earn documented service hours for mentoring younger athletes through the mental side of sport. We handle logistics, tracking, and matching. You get a turnkey peer mentorship program with zero overhead."

**What's in it for them:**
- Satisfies NCAA D1 mental health peer mentorship requirement
- Documented service hours for athletes (counts toward NCAA TeamWorks competition)
- Leadership development credential for mentors
- Zero cost, zero overhead for the athletic department

**What's in it for athletes:**
- Service hours credit
- Leadership experience
- Giving back to the sport
- NIL-compatible (paid mentoring is permissible under NIL rules if framed around personal story/expertise, not athletic performance — athletes must disclose deals over $600 to their institution)

---

## NCAA Compliance Notes

- No NCAA bylaw prohibits athletes from volunteering as mentors
- Paying mentors via NIL is likely permissible — tie compensation to personal story and expertise, not athletic performance
- Any formal AD partnership requires sign-off from compliance officer — build this into the process
- Service activities not involving athletic ability are permitted with prior institutional approval (low bar, compliance formality)
