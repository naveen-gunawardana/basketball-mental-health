# TikTok Content — Mentality Sports

Research-backed post ideas. Three buckets: athlete stories, resources, behind the platform.
TikTok algorithm actively favors mental health content — structural advantage for this brand.
Keep everything under 60 seconds. No polish. Realness beats production quality every time.

---

## Athlete Story / Relatable Content

**Post 1 — Talking Head (phone camera, gym or locker room wall)**
Hook: "Nobody told me this would be the hardest part of being an athlete."
Talk about the moment the mental side hit harder than anything physical. Specific — "I couldn't eat. Couldn't sleep. My body was ready. My mind wasn't. And I had no idea what to do with that."
> nobody prepares you for this part. the physical training is all laid out. the mental side? you're just supposed to figure it out alone. share this with an athlete who needs to hear it.
> #athletementalhealth #studentathlete #mentalityapp #sportsmentalhealth #athletelife

---

**Post 2 — POV Text Cards (dark background, no talking)**
Hook: "POV: You just got cut from the team."
Text cards progress through the moment — the drive home, telling your parents, walking into school the next day. The identity crisis that follows is what nobody talks about. End: "Your worth was never your stats."
> getting cut doesn't mean you're done. it means you've hit a wall most athletes never tell anyone about. you're not alone in this.
> #athletementalhealth #gettingcut #studentathlete #sportsidentity #mentality

---

**Post 3 — Voiceover over B-roll (empty gym, locker room, track)**
Hook: Sound of sneakers on gym floor. Text: "This is what burnout looks like for athletes."
"You still show up. You still put in the reps. But somewhere along the way you stopped feeling it. The game that used to be your escape started feeling like a job. You smile in the team photo. But inside you're just... done."
> burnout isn't quitting. it's what happens when no one teaches you how to protect your love for the game. have you felt this?
> #athleteburnout #sportsmentalhealth #studentathlete #mentality #athletelife

---

**Post 4 — Talking Head (direct to camera, one take energy)**
Hook: "I used to think needing help made me soft."
Break down where that belief comes from — the coach who said "run it off," the culture that equates toughness with silence. Flip it: "The strongest thing I ever did was talk to someone who had been through the same thing."
> the toughness culture in sports costs athletes more than anyone tracks. asking for help is not weakness. it is the hardest rep in the room.
> #athletementalhealth #toughness #mentality #sportsculture #mentalhealthmatters

---

**Post 5 — "Boom Clap" Reveal Format (trending audio)**
Hook: "I finally told someone I was struggling. Here's what happened."
Set up the fear — losing your spot, your coach's respect, your teammates' trust. Beat drops. Reveal: the person you told had been through the exact same thing. They didn't judge you. They helped you.
> you don't need someone with a clipboard. you need someone who played through the same thing you're going through. that's the whole idea behind mentality.
> #athletementalhealth #mentorship #mentality #sportspsychology #studentathlete

---

**Post 6 — Stitch Bait (direct address)**
Hook: "If you're an athlete and you've felt like this, stitch this."
Rapid-fire list of things athletes feel but don't say — scared to mess up in front of the team, comparing yourself to every athlete on your FYP, playing hurt because asking out feels like quitting, dreading a sport you used to love. Pause. Look at camera. "You're not broken. The system just never gave you the tools."
> every athlete on this list needed someone to talk to. we built the platform for that. comment which one hit hardest.
> #athletementalhealth #relatable #studentathlete #mentality #sportslife

## Resource / Tips

**Post 7 — Text on Screen + Voiceover (fast-paced cards)**
Hook: "3 things no one teaches athletes about mental performance."
1. Pre-competition anxiety is normal — elite athletes have it too, the goal is to channel it not eliminate it. 2. Rest is a mental performance tool, not weakness. 3. Who you are off the field directly affects who you are on it.
> they teach you how to train your body for years. nobody spends 10 minutes on this. share with a teammate.
> #sportspsychology #athletementalhealth #mentalperformance #mentality #studentathlete

---

**Post 8 — Talking Head (quick cuts, confident)**
Hook: "Stop telling athletes to just breathe."
Myth-bust generic mental health advice that doesn't work for competitive athletes. "Breathe. Journal. Meditate. None of that lands when you're in a locker room before the biggest game of your season. You need sport-specific tools."
> generic mental health advice was not built for athletes. we were.
> #athletementalhealth #sportspsychology #mentality #mentalperformance #athletecoach

---

**Post 9 — Text Cards (dark background, "Rein Me In" by Sam Fender audio)**
Hook: "The mental side of injury that nobody talks about."
Text cards: physical rehab is a plan. Mental rehab is invisible. You're still an athlete in your head but can't prove it with your body. Identity loss. Fear of coming back. Fear of being forgotten by the team.
> the physical injury heals. the mental side takes longer. have you been through this?
> #injuredathlete #athletementalhealth #sportinjury #mentality #athleterecovery

---

**Post 10 — Rapid-fire Talking Head (almost confrontational)**
Hook: "Here is what a sports mentor actually does. And it is not what you think."
Bust the assumption that mentors give motivational speeches. "They tell you what worked when their coach benched them. They tell you how they got through the season where everything fell apart. They've sat in the exact chair you're sitting in."
> a mentor who has been through it is different from a coach who has studied it. that's the foundation of mentality.
> #athletementor #mentorship #mentality #sportsmentalhealth #athletecoach

---

**Post 11 — "Because here's my thing…" Confessional Audio**
Hook: Lip-sync the audio, then cut to talking head.
"Athletes are told to leave everything on the field. But nobody tells you what to do when the field is the only place you feel like yourself. When practice ends and you don't know who you are for the next 18 hours. That's an identity problem. Not weakness."
> if the field is the only place you feel like you belong, that's worth talking about. not fixing. just talking.
> #athleteidentity #sportsmentalhealth #mentality #studentathlete #athletementalhealth

## Behind the Platform / Founder Content

**Post 12 — Talking Head (phone quality, no studio)**
Hook: "Why I built Mentality."
"I played sports my whole life. There were stretches where I was completely lost and had no one to talk to who actually got it. Not a therapist who had never played. Not a coach who only cared about performance. I needed someone who had been through the same thing and made it out. That person didn't exist for me. So I built the platform."
> every platform comes from a real problem. this one came from a real hurt. this is why mentality exists.
> #founderstory #mentality #athletementalhealth #buildinginpublic #sportsmentalhealth

---

**Post 13 — Behind the Scenes (screen recording or b-roll + voiceover)**
Hook: "Here's how we actually match athletes with mentors."
Walk through the matching concept simply. "We're not an app that throws you at a random sports psych. We match athletes with former players who've been through what you're going through. Same sport. Similar level. Similar pressure."
> we built this to be the resource i wish i had. here's how it works.
> #mentality #athletementor #buildinginpublic #sportsapp #athletementalhealth

---

**Post 14 — Mentor Talking Head (direct to camera)**
Hook: "I was a D1 athlete. And I was barely keeping it together."
A mentor on the platform tells their story in under 60 seconds. Specific, unpolished. End: "That's why I'm on Mentality. Because I know what it costs when you don't have someone in your corner who gets it."
> the mentors on this platform aren't here to motivate you. they're here because they've been exactly where you are.
> #mentality #athletementor #athletementalhealth #sportsmentalhealth #formerpro

**Post 15 — Comment Reply Video**

> this comment deserved a real answer. if you're in this spot right now, reach out. that's what we're here for.

## Execution Notes

- Post 3-5x per week minimum to build algorithmic momentum
- Reply to every comment in the first 30 minutes — early engagement velocity matters
- Keep everything under 60 seconds for the first 30 posts
- Jump on trending sounds within 3-5 days of them trending
- End every caption with a question, a directive, or a belonging statement
- One comment reply video per week doubles output at zero extra effort
- No polish needed — talking head in a gym parking lot outperforms studio every time for this audience
