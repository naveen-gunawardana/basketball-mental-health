# Competitor Analysis

## Overview

Research into how similar platforms structure their mentorship programs — session length, frequency, pricing, mentor requirements, onboarding. Use this to inform Mentality Sports product decisions.

---

## Athlete To Athlete
**Site:** [athletetoathlete.com](https://athletetoathlete.com)

### What They Do
Virtual mentorship pairing youth athletes (ages 8–18) with vetted college athletes from 200+ universities across 30+ sports. Not sport-specific — covers all youth sports.

### Session Structure
Three tiers:

| Tier | Ages | Length | Frequency | Price/session |
|------|------|--------|-----------|---------------|
| Basic | 8–13 | 30 min | Weekly or biweekly | $59–69 |
| Intermediate | 12–18 | 45 min | Weekly or biweekly | $79–89 |
| Advanced | 12–18 | 60 min | Weekly or biweekly | $99–109 |

Price drops with larger session packages (12 / 24 / 36 sessions). Unused sessions roll over. **First session is free.**

### What Sessions Cover
- Building confidence and motivation
- Goal setting and accountability
- Film review and technique
- Nutrition and fueling
- Rest and recovery routines
- College-level drills and exercises
- Handling setbacks and frustration

### How It Works
1. Parents submit athlete info (sport, position, goals)
2. Free intro meeting with matched mentor
3. Purchase a program → weekly/biweekly sessions begin

### Mentor Details
- College athletes, vetted, from 200+ universities
- **Paid per session** — mentors earn money

### Platform
- Google Meet
- Matching based on sport/position from signup form
- Sport/school preferences accommodated where possible

### Key Takeaways for Us
- **45-min sessions is the sweet spot** for our 12–18 age group (their Intermediate tier)
- Weekly or biweekly is the standard cadence — we should default to weekly, biweekly as fallback
- Paying mentors increases reliability and commitment — worth building into Phase 2 revenue model
- Free first session removes signup friction
- Rollover sessions reduce dropout from missed weeks — good mechanic to adopt
- They cover film review + goal setting as core session activities — these are concrete enough to give mentors structure without being overly prescriptive

## Athlete Experience
**Site:** [athlete-experience.com](https://athlete-experience.com)

### What They Do
Virtual 1:1 mentorship with college and professional athletes. Explicitly focused on the mental and performance side — areas "traditional coaching often misses." Has a Christian faith-based angle.

### Session Structure
- **Length: 40–45 minutes**
- **Scheduling: Completely flexible — mentee picks days and times**
- **Format:** Zoom or FaceTime
- **Onboarding:** Free 10-minute intro call before committing

### What Sessions Cover
- Mental performance and confidence
- Sports IQ and game preparation
- Technique and fundamentals
- Film study
- Leadership development
- Faith-based personal growth (their differentiator)
- Self-confidence and mindset

### How It Works
1. Fill out "Get Started" form
2. Team matches athlete to mentor
3. Free 10-min intro call
4. Select program tier
5. Begin weekly 1:1 virtual sessions

### Mentor Details
- College + professional athletes
- All mentors are Christian athletes (their niche)
- Competitive playing experience required

### Key Takeaways for Us
- **40–45 min is validated** as the right session length for this age group — consistent with Athlete To Athlete's Intermediate tier
- Flexible scheduling is a stated selling point — don't lock mentors/mentees into fixed slots
- **Free 10-min intro call** is a low-friction way to validate the match before committing
- Matching by the platform (not self-serve) builds trust and reduces mismatch
- Their faith angle is a niche differentiator — our basketball-specific angle serves the same function: it filters for people who actually want this specific thing

## What the Market Has Validated

1. **40–45 min sessions work** for the 12–18 age group (two independent platforms converged on this)
2. **Weekly cadence is standard**, biweekly as the flexible fallback
3. **Free intro call/first session** reduces signup friction significantly
4. **Flexible scheduling** is a selling point, not a nice-to-have
5. **Paying mentors** (even small amounts) improves reliability — Athlete To Athlete does this explicitly
6. **Matching by the platform** (not self-serve browsing) builds trust
7. **Structured session topics** (goal setting, film review, confidence) give mentors a framework — avoids "what do we even talk about?" drop-off
8. **Niche specificity** (basketball, Christian athletes, female athletes) is a moat — generic = noise

## Our Differentiation
- **Basketball-specific** — not 30 sports, just basketball
- **Mental side tied to real in-game problems** — playing time, coach conversations, game confidence — not generic wellness
- **SF grassroots network** — coach referrals, pilot relationships, local trust
- **Not therapy, not skills training** — a third category: peer mentorship from players who've been there
