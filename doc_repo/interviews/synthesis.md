## Interview Summaries

### Coach Tim (Freshman Coach)
**Role**: UHS Freshman basketball coach, multiple years experience  
**Roster**: 16 players, most equally skilled  
**Fit**: ☒ Champion — would actively push this

**What He Sees**:
- Players who can execute in practice won't even try in games
- Scrimmages against weaker lineups end in ties (should be blowouts)
- Freshman team culture is "least friendly" — less encouraging than JV/Varsity
- Only 3/16 players know how to advocate for themselves effectively although 10 have tried

**What He's Tried**:
- Tells players "make as many mistakes as possible"
- Doesn't sub players for missed shots/turnovers (only for not following game plan)

**Key Insight**: The problem isn't transition shock, skill plateaus, or role confusion. It's **self-imposed pressure**, **fear of mistakes**, and **peer culture**.

## Key Validated Problems

### 1. Practice-to-Game Translation / Confidence Gap (HIGH SEVERITY)
**Source**: Coach Tim, Jet

Players can execute in practice but don't perform in games. This isn't a skills issue—it's mental.

| Finding | Source |
|---------|--------|
| "I've seen students who I know can do stuff that they're not even trying" | Tim |
| Scrimmages that should be blowouts end up ties | Tim |
| "Missing a shot in game used to be really like confidence killer" | Jet |
| Plays different against friends vs. strangers ("environment thing") | Jet |

**Root Causes**:
- Fear of making mistakes
- Self-imposed pressure
- Peer pressure / lack of encouragement
- Nervousness against unfamiliar opponents

---

### 2. Fear of Making Mistakes (HIGH SEVERITY)
**Source**: Coach Tim

Players are so afraid to fail that they won't even try things they can do.

> "The students who are not getting better are the ones who are afraid to make mistakes. You're never going to learn how to do anything if you don't want to make a mistake."

Tim's approach: "Get out here and make as many mistakes as possible." But it's not working—players are still afraid despite supportive coaching.

---

### 3. Team Culture / Peer Pressure (HIGH SEVERITY)
**Source**: Coach Tim

Freshman team lacks the supportive culture of upper teams.

> "Our fresh team is least friendly out of all the different teams. We not as encouraging as the other teams. Varsity is more encouraging of mistakes."

- Trash talk among peers affects confidence
- Players like Leo and Yusuf impacted
- Needs intentional culture-building

---

### 4. Self-Advocacy Skills Gap (MEDIUM SEVERITY)
**Source**: Coach Tim

Players ask about playing time but don't know HOW to productively engage.

> "Only three know how to advocate for themselves and has actually said something and say what they can do to show or like try to get something out the deal."

- 10/16 players have asked about playing time
- Only 3/16 know how to advocate effectively
- **Success story**: Daniel asked, engaged, and improved ("been cooking lately")

---

### 5. Schedule / Balance Constraints (MEDIUM SEVERITY)
**Source**: Jacob, Jet

Time constraints limit development, especially for multi-sport athletes and long commuters.

| Player | Constraint |
|--------|------------|
| Jacob | Plays tennis + basketball + school |
| Jet | 1-1.5hr commute each way; can't train mornings |

Jet's workaround: Gym with dad, basketball homework from outside coach, film study during downtime.
