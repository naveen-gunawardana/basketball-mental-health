## 5. Explore Their Worldview (15 min)

*Go deep on schedule + confidence*

### Problem A: Schedule / Commute

**"Walk me through a typical day — when do you leave, when do you get home, what happens after?"**
Morning: stretching, eat food. After eating (around 10-11am on breaks): goes to gym with dad, lifts first, then court. At court: works on basketball homework from outside coach, or if courts are full, does ball handling on sideline or plays pickup. Then heads home, eats, afternoon family activities or film study on games/player studies.

**"How do you try to fit in extra work right now?"**
Works out with dad at gym, follows basketball homework from outside coach. Uses any available time.

**"What's frustrating about that?"**
Commute takes time away from training AND from getting homework done (which would free up more time to train).

**"How often do you feel like you're falling behind teammates who have more time?"**
- ☐ Daily ☒ Weekly ☐ Occasionally

**"What have you tried to fix it?"**
Maximizes training time, does morning stretching, schedules gym time with dad, studies film during downtime.

**"How does it affect you — energy, motivation, performance?"**
Still maintains very high motivation - uses basketball as stress reliever. Seems to have adapted well despite constraints.

---

### Problem B: Confidence / Playing Passive

**"Walk me through a game where you felt like you held back when you shouldn't have."**
Reardon game (they won): "I felt like I wasn't being aggressive enough. And when I did have the chance... I missed. I got really down on myself... I wasn't sure like why I'm not doing this or doing that... I felt like I wasn't helping the team as much as I wanted to."

**"What goes through your head in those moments?"**
Questioning himself - "I don't get like what I wasn't like I wasn't sure like why I'm not like doing this or doing that"

**"How do you try to deal with it right now?"**
After games: reflects on what happened, how he feels, how game went. Tries to come back stronger. In game: takes a breather, remembers "it's going to be okay, there's going to be more opportunities."

**"How often does this happen?"**
- ☐ Every game ☒ Most games ☐ Sometimes

**"What have you tried to be more aggressive?"**
Working on confidence, trusting himself even if he misses. "Being in the moment." Actively trying to be more aggressive this season (noted by interviewer that he's seemed more confident/aggressive).

**"How does it affect you after — do you think about it?"**
Yes - always reflects after games. Uses it to improve next time.

## 6. Dig Deeper — Practice vs. Games

*Understand the confidence gap*

> "You said you can do things in practice that don't show up in games. Why do you think that is?"

**Response:**
"It's kind of an environment thing. When I'm playing against my friends like in our team, it's just kind of a different vibe."

> "What's different about how you feel in practice vs. games?"

**Response:**
Playing against people he doesn't know can be "nerve racking at first" but acknowledges "part of the growth is just kind of getting over that."

> "Is there anything that helps you play more like yourself?"

**Response:**
Being patient. Taking a breather. Remembering there will be more opportunities. "Really about being patient for me."

---

## 7. Dig Deeper — Mentorship

*Test appetite for mentor relationship*

> "Have you ever talked to an older player — like a college guy or someone who played at a high level — about stuff you're going through?"

**Response:**
Has an outside coach - trains with him every Sunday (1 hour sessions). Sometimes chats with him after training. Also messages him through his dad for advice.

> "If you could have a weekly 15-minute call with someone who played college ball, just to talk through what you're working on, would you use it?"

**Response:**
"Definitely. I think talking to someone who's gone through the long journey is very helpful for a young player."

> "What would you want to talk about — skills, confidence, schedule, something else?"

**Response:**
"Specifically... mentally. Because basketball... it can be stressful. It can be joyful. It has a lot of emotions packed into it and I think having a mentor in basketball can be really helpful if you're talking about it with someone."
