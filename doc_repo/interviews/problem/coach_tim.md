# Problem Interview: Coach Tim (Freshman Coach)

## Interview Details
- **Date**: January 2026
- **Interviewee**: Coach Tim
- **Role/Background**: UHS Freshman basketball coach (multiple years)
- **How Found**: Naveen's coaching network

---

> "Hey Tim, appreciate you taking the time. I'm working on something to help high school basketball players with the mental side — staying motivated, understanding their role, dealing with the grind. Before I build anything, I want to understand what you actually see as a coach. Not selling anything — just trying to learn from someone in the trenches."

**Transition**: "Let me start with a few quick questions about your coaching."

## 3. Tell a Story (2 min)

> "I've been talking to coaches and players, and I keep hearing about freshmen who come in hyped, work hard, but then hit a wall — maybe they're not getting minutes, or they don't see improvement, or they just don't know how to handle the jump from middle school. Does that match what you see?"

**Notes** (initial reaction):
Doesn't see transition shock or motivation drop. What he DOES see: players adding pressure to themselves, struggling to translate practice skills to games, and lacking confidence. "I've seen students who I know can do stuff that they're not even trying."

---

|---------|------------|-----------|-------|
| **Transition shock** — Players not ready for HS speed/intensity. Confidence crashes. | 4 | ☐ Yes ☒ No | "I have not seen students not be ready for sports" - it's self-imposed pressure, not transition shock |

**Other problems you see with freshmen specifically**:
- **Practice-to-game translation problem**: "It's a translation thing where the students are not putting what they do in practice into play"
- **Fear of making mistakes**: Players are "afraid to make mistakes" which prevents learning
- **Team culture/encouragement**: "Our fresh team is least friendly out of all the different teams... not as encouraging as other teams"
- **Peer pressure**: Trash talk and lack of peer support affects confidence

---

## 5. Deep Dive — Top Problems (15 min)

*Go deep on the top 2 problems*

### Problem A: Practice-to-Game Translation / Confidence

**"What does this look like when it happens?"**
Players can do things in practice but don't even try in games. "I've seen students who I know can do stuff that they're not even trying." In scrimmages, any starting five should dominate but they tie - "this doesn't make sense."

**"Can you tell me about a specific player this happened to?"**
Daniel - came to coach asking why he wasn't getting playing time. After that conversation, he "actually stepped it up a lot" and has "been cooking lately." Success story of a player who advocated and improved.

**"How do you handle it right now?"**
Tells players: "Get out here and make as many mistakes as possible. That's the only way you're going to learn." Tries to create safe environment - doesn't sub players out for missed shots or turnovers, only for not following game plan (e.g., shooting mid-range when team shoots threes).

**"What's frustrating about that approach?"**
Players are still afraid despite the supportive approach. "They so afraid because I don't know if it's like a pressure from their own peers."

**"How often — daily, weekly, couple times a season?"**
Ongoing issue - sees it every game when scrimmage results don't match competitive practice results.

**"What's the cost if you don't address it?"**
Games are closer than they should be; players don't develop because they won't take risks.

---

### Problem B: Team Culture / Peer Encouragement

**"What does this look like when it happens?"**
"Our fresh team is least friendly out of all the different teams. We not as encouraging as the other teams. Varsity is more encouraging of mistakes. JV is more encouraging."

**"Can you tell me about a specific player?"**
Leo and Yusuf - affected by the culture. Leo "got the skill to do the stuff and has all the tools" but culture/trash talk impacts his confidence. "I listen to the trash talk."

**"How do you handle it now?"**
Recognizes it's something he and Sully need to "build a little bit more intentionally." Acknowledges it "could be personalities" that may shift as players move up.

**"What's frustrating?"**
Contributing to confidence issues. Creates environment where players are afraid to make mistakes in front of peers.

**"Frequency?"**
Consistent issue throughout the season.

**"Cost?"**
Players with skill aren't performing; confidence suffers; game results don't match practice potential.

## 6. Coach-Player Communication

> "Do freshmen ever come to you to talk about their role, their development, stuff they're struggling with?"

**Response:**
Yes - about 10 out of 16 players have asked about playing time. "All the students ask in about playing time because they want to know what they can do."

> "What percentage want to ask but don't?"

**Response:**
Most do ask, but quality of asking varies significantly.

> "Why don't they?"

**Response:**
Not that they don't ask - it's that they don't know HOW to ask productively. "I don't think players know how to advocate for themselves. Because this is fresh... only three know how to advocate for themselves and has actually said something and say what they can do to show or like try to get something out the deal."

> "What would make those conversations more likely?"

**Response:**
Players need to learn how to advocate - not just ask "how can I get more play time?" but actually engage with what they can do to earn it.

---

## 7. Mentorship Concept

> "Have you ever thought about connecting your players with older players — like college guys or former HS players — to talk through stuff?"

**Response:**
Immediately receptive. Compared it to existing programs: "It reminds me of like our PAs... It's almost like a PA approach."

> "If there was a program where freshmen could have a weekly 15-min check-in with a college player who's been through it — would that be useful?"

**Response:**
"Nothing" would be needed to get him to join. "Anybody who loves sports will do this type of thing because they always want to be around sports and talk sports."

> "What would you want them to work on in those conversations?"

**Response:**
"Building confidence and have a healthy mentality towards playing sports."

> "What would make you hesitant?"

**Response:**
Nothing - fully supportive. Gave concrete advice on how to implement.
