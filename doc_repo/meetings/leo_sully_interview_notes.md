# Meeting Notes: Leo & Sully Interviews

## Quick Summary

**Leo** = Player struggling with confidence, self-criticism, unmet expectations (expected JV, got frosh)

**Sully** = Assistant coach/mentor who had 2 ACL injuries + tried sports psych (didn't work) + has organic mentors (did work)

---

## Key Takeaways to Mention

### 1. The "Sully Type" Mentor is What Players Want

Leo explicitly said he prefers talking to Sully because:
- "In between a coach and a player"
- "More my level... a little bit more of my age level"
- Tim is "Tim" (implied less approachable)
- Bill is "old school baller" (respects him but not same connection)

**Implication**: Mentors should be age-proximate (college players, recent grads) — not just coaches or formal psychologists.

---

### 2. Sports Psych vs. Organic Mentors (Sully's Experience)

Sully had a sports psychologist freshman year. **It didn't work.**

> "It felt like I was kind of just talking to him rather than thinking through stuff with him... empty space, empty conversations."

What DID work — his organic mentors (brother, trainer, Jackie):
- They were **thinking WITH him**, not just listening
- They trained together (not just talk)
- They helped with **life beyond basketball**

**Implication**: The product needs to be collaborative problem-solving, not passive listening. And it should extend beyond just basketball.

---

### 3. Leo's Core Issue: Self-Criticism Loop

- Fumbles a pass → turnover → "I feel like I let down my teammates" → gets mad at himself
- Tries harder BUT also gets down on himself (dual response that doesn't resolve)
- Teammate comments compound it: "I'll mess up and someone will say something and I'll just like get like, oh shit"

Sully's advice that's helping Leo: **"Next shot mentality"** + **"giving yourself a little more grace"**

---

### 4. Unmet Expectations Are Real

Leo expected to make JV, ended up on frosh.

> "It's definitely different at UHS... a lot more competitive... kind of lowered my confidence."

Also dealing with a position change (small forward → center) which "changed my ability."

---

### 5. Team Culture Issue: Trash Talk vs. Hype Imbalance

Sully observed:
> "Trash talk is fine, but then there's not enough hype as well. Specifically like practices... definitely some kids are trash talking, but then not saying anything positive."

Games have hype (winning by 20-40). Practice doesn't.

---

### 6. Role Confusion is Constant

Sully: "Minutes is all the time."

- Leo: small forward → center
- Joe, Story, Daniel: talked to them about shooting more

Sully's perspective: "No one really ever figures out as a basketball player... you're constantly changing, getting injured, getting stronger, getting taller."
