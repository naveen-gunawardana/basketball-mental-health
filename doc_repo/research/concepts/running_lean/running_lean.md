# Running Lean

A systematic process for iterating from Plan A to a plan that works, before running out of resources.

## Core Philosophy

- **Life's too short to build something nobody wants**
- Document your Plan A, then systematically de-risk it
- Speed of learning > speed of building
- Talk to customers before writing code

## The 3-Stage Process

### Stage 1: Document Your Plan A
Create a [Lean Canvas](./lean_canvas.md) capturing your initial hypotheses across 9 blocks. Takes ~20 minutes. Don't overthink—this is your starting point, not final plan.

### Stage 2: Identify the Riskiest Parts
Prioritize risks in this order:

1. **Problem/Solution Fit** (Is there a problem worth solving?)
   - Do customers acknowledge the problem?
   - How are they solving it today?
   - Are they willing to pay for a better solution?

2. **Product/Market Fit** (Have you built something people want?)
   - Does your MVP deliver on the UVP?
   - Are customers using and paying?
   - Is there evidence of retention/growth?

3. **Scale** (How do you accelerate growth?)
   - Can you sustain the business model?
   - Are unit economics viable?

### Stage 3: Systematically Test Your Plan

**Problem Interviews** → Validate problem exists and matters
- 10-15 conversations
- Don't pitch—listen
- Understand existing alternatives and willingness to pay

**Solution Interviews** → Validate solution resonates
- Demo/prototype before building
- Test pricing early
- Get commitment (pre-orders, LOIs, sign-ups)

**MVP Interviews** → Validate with working product
- Build minimum viable product
- Measure behavior, not just opinions
- Iterate based on usage data

## Key Metrics to Track

- **Acquisition**: How do users find you?
- **Activation**: Do they have a great first experience?
- **Retention**: Do they come back?
- **Revenue**: Are they paying?
- **Referral**: Do they tell others?

## Decision Framework

After each experiment:
- **Pivot**: Fundamental hypothesis was wrong—change direction
- **Persevere**: Hypothesis validated—double down
- **Iterate**: Small adjustments needed—refine approach
