## Platform Vision

### Core Concept
A **basketball-focused mentorship hub** connecting young athletes with college/pro players to support them through athlete struggles.

**Why it matters:** Learning from coaches and older players who've played at high levels provides advice through shared experiences. Start with basketball, then expand.

### Website Components
1. **Resource Hub** – Clean design with different personas
2. **Athlete Storytelling** – Reduce stigma of mental health in/out of sports
3. **Workshops & Education**

### Reference Sites
- [The Hidden Opponent](https://www.thehiddenopponent.org)
- [Athletes for Hope - Causeway](https://www.athletesforhope.org/what-we-do/causeway/) – good example of workshops/education
- [Morgan's Message - Education Program](https://www.morgansmessage.org/education-program) – ambassadors meeting virtually

## Mentorship Program Design

### Structure
- Mentors matched with a few players each
- Meet weekly or bi-weekly (based on needs)
- Waitlist/pairing process for overflow
- Application (not competitive) – just to learn about you

### Additional Content Ideas

- Notable speaker sessions

## MVP Plan

### Approach
Think of it as a **research project** – start small to validate

### MVP Stack
- **Intake:** Google Forms (questionnaire for both mentees & mentors)
- **Storage:** Google Sheets
- **Matching:** Manual, over email
- **Meetings:** Google Meet
- **Notes:** Shared doc with basic info, record conversations

### Pilot Size
- 2 students
- 2 mentors

### Market Research
Reach out to a few people – **5 questions:**
- Coach
    What would it take to be worth it?

- What do mentors need?


- Players 
    - What are the top problems they need help with
    - How do they identify problems?
