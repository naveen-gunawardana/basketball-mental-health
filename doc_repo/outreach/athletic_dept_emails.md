## 1. Therese Pitman — University of Pittsburgh

**To:** tpitman@athletics.pitt.edu
**Subject:** Peer Mentorship for Pitt Student-Athletes — Service Hours + Mental Health

Hi Therese,

My name is Naveen, and I'm a high school student in San Francisco and the founder of Mentality Sports.

Mentality Sports connects college athletes and former athletes with high school athletes for one-on-one conversations about the mental side of competing. That means things like confidence, anxiety, pressure, injury, and handling adversity. The goal is to give younger athletes someone they can actually talk to who has been through it themselves, not a therapist or a coach with a different agenda, but a peer who understands the experience from the inside.

I'm reaching out because I think Pitt student-athletes could be a great fit as mentors. Sessions are documented, so the hours count as community service, and it's also a meaningful leadership experience for the mentors themselves. For schools at the D1 level, it also aligns with the NCAA requirement that went into effect in 2024 around peer-to-peer mental health support.

If this seems like something worth exploring, I'd love to set up a short call and share more.
