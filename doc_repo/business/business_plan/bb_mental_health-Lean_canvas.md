### Personas

#### Jet — Schedule conflict + confidence issues

Jet is a committed player who genuinely wants to improve, but geography works against him. He lives out in Richmond while attending school in SF, and that 1 hour 15 minute commute each way eats into everything.

- **Commute:** 1h15m each way from Richmond, CA — over 2.5 hours of his day gone before he touches a basketball
- **No morning workouts:** By the time he could get to a gym, he'd be late for school. Teammates who live 15 minutes away hit 24 Hour Fitness before first period.
- **Homework stacks up:** His school is academically demanding. After practice ends at 6pm and the commute home, he's doing homework until midnight. No energy left to train.
- **Falling behind:** He watches teammates get extra reps in while his schedule won't allow it. The gap grows week by week.
- **Confidence gap:** Jet has the skills — you can see it in practice. But in games, he plays passive. Hesitates on drives he should take. Doesn't attack when he's open. The physical tools are there, but something mental holds him back.


#### Ethan — Decision-making + unclear role

Ethan is the kind of player coaches love to have on the roster. He shows up early, stays late, and never complains. His skills are solid — he can shoot, he can defend, he understands the game. But when the lights come on, something breaks down.

- **Hard worker:** First in the gym, last to leave. Does extra shooting after practice. Watches film on his own.
- **Good skills:** Technically sound. Can execute in drills and scrimmages. Coaches know he has ability.
- **Decision-making struggles:** In games, he rushes. Makes the extra pass when he should shoot. Shoots when he should pass. Reads develop a half-second too slow.
- **Unclear role compounds it:** He gets maybe 5 minutes a game. When he checks in, he knows he has limited time to prove himself — so he presses. Tries to do too much. Forces plays that aren't there.
- **The spiral:** Limited minutes → desperation to make an impact → bad decisions → back to the bench → confidence drops → even worse decisions next time
- **Needs deeper interview:** What's driving the hesitation? Is it the role uncertainty, fear of mistakes, or something else? TBD.

## Problem
*Top problems for SF high school basketball players (by priority)*

### P0 — Focus Areas
1. **Skill plateaus + no progress tracking (42)** — Without clear progress tracking, effort feels pointless and motivation erodes. "Just work harder" feedback doesn't help.
2. **Unclear roles + volatile playing time (42)** — Deep rosters where minutes feel arbitrary reduce confidence and persistence; players don't know how to talk to coaches, fear retaliation.

### P1 — Secondary
- **School/Schedule Conflict (49)** — Travel teams + demanding academics reduce sleep and raise stress.
- **Controlling coaching (40)** — "Do it my way or sit" reduces autonomy → mental checkout.

### P2–P3 — Lower Priority
- Verbal/emotional abuse from win-at-all-costs coaching (36)
- Social media comparison makes normal development feel like failure (40)
- Parent/Coach sideline conflict (35)
- Reporting friction (28)

### Existing Alternatives
- More trainer sessions; random drills from YouTube; occasional camps
- Parents talk to coach; switching teams (costly)
- Late-night homework; sacrificing social time; tutoring
- Endure it; seek a mentor coach; rely on friends for support
- Generic therapy apps (Headspace, BetterHelp) — not sport-specific
- [The Hidden Opponent](https://www.thehiddenopponent.org) — advocacy but no direct mentorship

## Unique Value Proposition
*Single, clear, compelling message*

**Basketball-specific mentorship from players who've been there — weekly 1:1 guidance from college/pro athletes who understand the grind.**

### High-Level Concept
"Big Brothers Big Sisters for better basketball performance through mental health"

---

## Solution
*Top 3 features addressing P0 problems*

1. **Progress tracking tools** — Clear skill development metrics (shot %, handle control, weekly micro-tests) so effort feels visible and motivation sustains. Breaks skill plateaus.
2. **Mentorship matching** — Pair young players with college/pro mentors for weekly or bi-weekly check-ins on navigating playing time, understanding your role, and having coach conversations
3. **Resource Hub** — Coach conversation scripts, role clarity frameworks, dealing with benching, athlete storytelling
